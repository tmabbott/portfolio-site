Airbnb Announces Ukraine Relief Campaign
Hospitality company pledges short-term housing for 100,000 Ukrainian refugees

SAN FRANCISCO, CA (February 28, 2022) – Airbnb on Monday committed to making short-term housing available for up to 100,000 Ukrainians displaced by the Russo-Ukrainian war, which began February 24, 2022 following Russia’s invasion of Ukraine. The conflict has so far exiled more than one million Ukrainians, with this number expected to grow significantly.

The stays are being funded by Airbnb and by donors to a newly created Airbnb.org Refugee Program.

	•	Airbnb hosts have the option to offer free or discounted stays for refugees, with remaining costs covered by Airbnb
	•	Airbnb is waiving host and guest fees for all refugee stays
	•	Airbnb has partnered with over 40 nonprofit organizations worldwide for campaign support
	•	Airbnb supports refugee guests regardless of nationality, race, ethnicity, or how they identify

“We have reached out to governments in Poland, Germany, Hungary, and Romania, and other countries to offer assistance with housing Ukrainian refugees. With funding support from corporate and non-profit partners and the assistance of hosts who list their properties with us, we are prepared to house up to 100,000 refugees.” - Airbnb CEO Brian Chesky

Airbnb was born in 2007 when two hosts welcomed three guests to their San Francisco home, and has since grown to over 5 million hosts who have welcomed over 1.5 billion guest arrivals in almost every country across the globe. Every day, hosts offer unique stays and experiences that make it possible for guests to connect with communities in a more authentic way.
