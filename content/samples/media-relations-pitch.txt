Subject line: Airbnb pledges short-term housing relief for Ukrainian refugees

February 28, 2022

Hi Molly,

I’m reaching out to let you know about Airbnb’s new program to temporarily house up to 100,000 Ukrainian refugees. The Ukraine Relief Campaign follows Russia’s recent invasion of Ukraine, which has displaced an estimated one million Ukranians–a number that is expected to grow exponentially.

Our CEO and campaign donor Brian Chesky is available to you to discuss the details of this housing relief campaign. Our hope is to get this news out to refugee relief programs, government workers, airbnb hosts, and refugees in need of housing as quickly and widely as possible. Campaign details include:

	•	Housing assistance for up to 100,000 refugees, at no cost to them.
	•	Funding provided by Airbnb and by donations to the Relief Fund
	•	Hosts can offer a discounted rate to refugees with Airbnb covering all costs.
	•	All host and guest fees will be waived for refugee stays.

As a reporter covering the hospitality industry, we hope you will consider writing a story and helping us spread the word about this program. More information on this campaign can be found at https://www.airbnb.org/help-ukraine.
